The Stranger's Bargain
A man sits beside the trail with a lame mule and a wagon full of medicine. He calls himself a doctor, though his hands shake and his coat is stained with something darker than mud. Mary's cholera is worsening — she hasn't kept water down since yesterday. The doctor says he can treat her, but his price is steep: sixty pounds of food, or all your remaining medicine and $200 cash. He nods toward William. "Your man there doesn't look well either. I'd act before the river crossing."
