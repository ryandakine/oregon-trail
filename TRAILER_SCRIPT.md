# Oregon Trail AI — Trailer Script

**Format:** 38-second muted-autoplay hero video (16:9, 1920x1080)
**Style:** Stylized 2D watercolor animation — warm golden tones desaturating to ashen grays
**Tone arc:** Hopeful Americana -> dark humor -> deadpan horror -> punchline

Why watercolor: Pixel art screams "retro clone." Live-action AI video still looks uncanny. Watercolor is immediately distinctive, evokes period-appropriate frontier illustration, and the style degradation (lush to washed-out to almost-monochrome) carries the tonal escalation visually without a single word of audio. It also generates well in Sora/Runway — painterly styles mask imperfections that realistic prompts expose.

## Part 1: Shot-by-Shot Script

### SHOT 1 — "The Departure" (0:00–0:04)
**Duration:** 4 seconds
**Visual:** Wide establishing shot. A covered wagon rolls out of Independence, Missouri at golden hour. Wildflowers line the road. A family of five walks alongside the wagon — the father tips his hat, a daughter waves at someone off-screen. Two oxen pull steadily. Everything glows.
**Text overlay:** None (let the beauty land for 2 seconds, then:)
**Text overlay (fade in at 0:02):** `Every journey begins with hope.`
**Mood:** Warm strings, Aaron Copland Americana. If muted: the visuals do all the work.
**Transition:** Slow dissolve to Shot 2.

### SHOT 2 — "The Promise" (0:04–0:08)
**Duration:** 4 seconds
**Visual:** Medium shot of the family making camp at dusk. Father marks a journal by firelight. Mother pours coffee from a tin pot. Kids play near the wagon. Stars emerge. The watercolor palette is rich — deep blues, warm fire oranges.
**Text overlay:** `An AI dungeon master writes your story.`
**Mood:** Still warm but the music (if present) drops slightly. Intimacy.
**Transition:** Match cut — the campfire flickers, and we cut to:

---

### SHOT 3 — "First Trouble" (0:08–0:12)
**Duration:** 4 seconds
**Visual:** The wagon fords a river. Water is up to the axles. The mother holds a child above the waterline, face tense. The palette shifts cooler — steel blue water, overcast sky. One supply crate floats away downstream, clearly lost. The father reaches for it and misses.
**Text overlay:** `Grounded in real 1848 history.`
**Mood:** First shift. The golden tones are gone. This is harder country.
**Transition:** Smash cut to Shot 4 (abrupt — the first "something's wrong" beat).

---

### SHOT 4 — "The Diagnosis" (0:12–0:16)
**Duration:** 4 seconds
**Visual:** Close-up of a hand-drawn terminal screen (the actual game UI rendered in the watercolor style). Amber text on dark background reads: `Sarah has contracted cholera.` Below it, the party roster shows health bars — Sarah's is dropping in real time. The cursor blinks.
**Text overlay:** `No two playthroughs are the same.`
**Mood:** The watercolors are fading. Less saturated. The campfire from Shot 2 is gone — this is cold daylight. Deadpan.
**Transition:** Quick dissolve to Shot 5.

---

### SHOT 5 — "The Gravestone" (0:16–0:20)
**Duration:** 4 seconds
**Visual:** A wooden grave marker on an empty prairie. Wind blows through dead grass. The inscription reads (legible): `Sarah Henderson / Age 7 / "She asked for water / we had none to give."` The family stands behind it — now four people. The daughter who was waving in Shot 1 is missing. The father's hat is in his hands.
**Text overlay:** `Every death is permanent.`
**Mood:** The watercolor palette is nearly monochrome — pale ochre, ash gray, faded sepia. Stillness.
**Transition:** Hard cut to Shot 6.

---

### SHOT 6 — "The Descent" (0:20–0:26)
**Duration:** 6 seconds
**Visual:** Rapid montage (3 quick cuts, 2 seconds each):

**6A (0:20–0:22):** Terminal screen in watercolor style. Text reads: `Thomas has not spoken in four days. At supper he divided his ration into two portions and left one on the ground.` The cursor blinks after each line.

**6B (0:22–0:24):** The remaining family trudges through a mountain pass in a blizzard. The wagon is listing. One ox is missing. The color palette is almost entirely gray and white. The son walks slightly behind the others — alone, head down, carrying something in his hand that the viewer can't quite identify.

**6C (0:24–0:26):** Terminal screen. Game UI shows the party roster. The son's sanity stat reads `12/100`. Below the choices: `Before you can decide, Thomas acts —` The text types itself out. No player input.

**Text overlay (across all three cuts):**
- 6A: `Three tone tiers.`
- 6B: `Classroom-safe.`
- 6C: `To psychological horror.`

**Mood:** The whiplash between "classroom-safe" appearing over increasingly disturbing visuals IS the joke. Deadpan mismatch.
**Transition:** Smash cut to black.

## Part 3: Text Overlay Specs

### Typography
- **Primary text** (narrative overlays): `IBM Plex Mono` or `JetBrains Mono`, 600 weight, 36px at 1080p
- **Terminal text** (game UI inserts): `IBM Plex Mono`, 400 weight, 24px at 1080p, amber `#FFB000` on black `#0A0A0A`
- **Newspaper text**: `Playfair Display` or `EB Garamond`, 700 weight, serif, dark brown `#2C1810` on sepia `#F4E4C1`
- **CTA text** (final screen): `IBM Plex Mono`, 500 weight, 48px for URL, 28px for tagline

### Text Shadow / Readability
All non-terminal text overlays get a subtle shadow: `2px 2px 8px rgba(0,0,0,0.8)` to ensure readability over the watercolor backgrounds. White or near-white text: `#F5F0E8` (warm white, not pure white — matches the watercolor palette).

### Video File Specs

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Resolution | 1920x1080 (1080p) |
| Codec | H.264 (MP4) for broad compatibility |
| Bitrate | 4-6 Mbps (watercolor style compresses well) |
| File size target | Under 8 MB for the 38-second loop |
| Fallback | `poster="/trailer-poster.jpg"` — frame from Shot 1 (the hopeful departure) |
| Accessibility | `aria-label="Promotional trailer for Oregon Trail AI game"` on the video element |

### Performance Considerations

- `preload="auto"` is fine — 8 MB is negligible and the trailer IS the first impression
- Serve from Cloudflare Pages alongside the static assets (same origin, no CORS)
- The `playsinline` attribute is required for iOS autoplay
- Safari requires `muted` for autoplay — already specified
- If bandwidth is a concern, add a `<source>` with a 720p WebM as fallback:

```html
<video autoplay muted loop playsinline poster="/trailer-poster.jpg">
  <source src="/trailer.webm" type="video/webm">
  <source src="/trailer.mp4" type="video/mp4">
```

## Part 5: Production Sequence

### Step 1: Generate watercolor scenes (Sora or Runway)
Generate each scene from the 7 prompts above. Expect 2-3 generations per scene to get a usable take. Total: ~15-20 generations.

### Step 2: Screen-record terminal scenes
Run the actual game (or mock the UI) and record Shots 4, 6A, 6C, and 7 as screen captures. The game's real terminal aesthetic is better than any AI approximation.

### Step 3: Composite in video editor
- CapCut (free), DaVinci Resolve (free), or even Canva video editor
- Layer watercolor scenes as the base
- Overlay terminal screen recordings onto the Prompt 7 watercolor frame
- Add text overlays per the timing table
- Add transitions (dissolves and hard cuts as specified)

### Step 4: Color grade the arc
- Shots 1-2: Warm, saturated, golden
- Shot 3: Cool, desaturated 30%
- Shot 4-5: Desaturated 60%, cooler
- Shot 6: Nearly monochrome, cold
- Shot 7: Pure black (no grading needed)
- Shot 8: Sepia warmth returns (but different — aged, not golden)
- Shot 9: Black with amber accent

This gradient is the spine of the trailer. If you only do one post-production pass, make it this one.

### Step 5: Export and compress
- Export 1080p H.264, CRF 23 (good balance of quality and size)
- Run through `ffmpeg -i trailer_raw.mp4 -c:v libx264 -crf 23 -preset slow -c:a none -movflags +faststart trailer.mp4`
- The `-movflags +faststart` flag moves the moov atom to the front for instant browser playback
- Verify file size is under 8 MB

## Part 6: Why This Works

**The escalation is the entire joke.** The first 8 seconds could be a tourism ad. By second 26, a mother is setting a place at the fire for her dead daughter. The text overlays maintain a calm, almost clinical tone throughout — the dissonance between the deadpan copy and the increasingly horrible visuals is what makes it funny AND memorable.

**It works muted.** Every beat is communicated through text overlays and visual storytelling. Audio would enhance but is not required. This is critical for autoplay, social feeds, and the landing page.

**It's shareable on its own.** Even without context about the game, the 38-second video tells a complete story: hope, trouble, loss, descent, dark punchline. "Most of them are obituaries" is the line people will quote.

**It sells the three things that matter:**
1. AI-generated (every playthrough unique)
2. Historically grounded (not a meme game)
3. Tonal range (the darkness is the hook)

**It does NOT oversell.** No feature lists. No "powered by Claude." No tech flex. Just: here's what happens when you play. The URL. Go.
